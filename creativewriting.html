Beginning

It started in the same place it ended: a forest in a place even I can’t remember. Trees, rivers, endless greens, and a man, asleep, somewhere in between. Freshly awoken, unsure of whether he was beginning or ending, he took a step, and then another. Blackness surrounding the fish at his feet, he bent down, catching one with his eyes.

“Hello. Hello little fish,” the man offered the fish. With great weight, she looked at the man. Opening her mouth, closing it, and opening it once more—as fish normally do, the man remembered—she spoke to him.

“What are you doing here, skin wherein I sleep, eat, and find love?” The man thought about it for a moment, closing his eyes in thought. The world around him—the breathing trees, with greens falling like rain preparing for a rainbow, the river rocks, with multitudes of moss, and the little flowers, the ever conquering mushrooms, the orange sunlight beaming unobtrusively from above—it all disappeared.

Alone in a place so familiarily unfamiliar to him, he could see himself in the in the endless black. He moved himself towards himself. With enough black behind him—and plenty more ahead—he turned to himself, and thought, “What am I doing here?”

This question troubled him.

What was he doing there after all? What right had he to step in the place where fish found love, foxes fought, and frogs flicked tongues in a flippant act of defiance against the toads? A man who knew not whether he was beginning or ending, as he was, in a place so perfect? With these questions from the mind in mind, the man saw himself begin to cry.

“I’m sorry sir, I meant not to alarm you.” The fish spoke. First confronted by the song of birds, he opened his eyes.

He then remembered what he was doing there, and he replied to the fish, “I’m talking to the fish, looking at the moss, and feeling the earth beneath my feet.”

“Very good.” The fish swam away, and the man fell beneath the water.

There he laid, having found the answer to whether he was beginning or ending.
